# Monetary Policy & Bank Risk Analysis

## Overview
This project investigates the relationship between monetary policy and bank risk-taking behaviour in Australia’s Big Four banks using econometric modelling.

## Objective
To determine whether changes in the cash rate influence bank risk levels, measured using Z-scores.

## Tools & Technologies
- Python (pandas, matplotlib, scikit-learn)
- Jupyter Notebook
- Econometric modelling (OLS regression)

## Methodology
- Cleaned and analysed banking and macroeconomic data
- Constructed an OLS regression model
- Included variables such as:
  - Cash rate
  - GDP growth
  - Inflation
  - Capital ratios
  - Cost-to-income ratio

## Key Results
- Found a statistically significant relationship between cash rate and bank risk
- Model achieved R² ≈ 0.56–0.61
- Results suggest that:
  - Lower interest rates are associated with increased bank risk-taking
  - Higher rates correspond to increased risk aversion

## Visualisations
Include:
- Correlation matrix
- Regression plots
- Residual diagnostics

## Business Implications
- Monetary policy has a measurable impact on institutional risk behaviour
- Banks should adjust risk management strategies during easing cycles
- Relevant for policymakers and financial institutions
